Metro Lost & Found

Problem Statement

The current metro lost and found system is slow, confusing, and hard to navigate. Users face delays and poor tracking, leading to frustration and ineffectiveness.

Design Solution

The Metro Lost and Found project streamlines reporting and retrieving lost items through a user-friendly platform, offering real-time updates and efficient tracking to enhance passenger convenience.

Secondary Research

  • The secondary research began with studying research papers, articles/journals relevant to the topic.
  • Further steps involved the literature review which helped to understand the problem better & analyse on the basis of other people's work.

Goals

  • Professional: Ravi aims to become a senior developer in the next few years.
  • Efficiency: He uses daily metro rides to maximise productivity with podcasts, emails, and articles.
  • Personal Development: Ravi enjoys learning new skills, including coding languages and tech trends.

Pain Points

  • Lost Belongings: Ravi worries about losing important items like his laptop bag or headphones.
  • Inefficient Recovery: He dislikes the manual process of recovering lost items.
  • Delays & Inefficiency: Metro delays frustrate him, disrupting his schedule.
  • Cluttered Apps: Ravi dislikes apps that are poorly designed or overly complex.

Needs

  • User-Friendly App: Ravi wants an easy-to-use lost & found app.
  • Instant Notifications: He prefers real-time updates on delays and lost items.
  • Quick Resolutions: Ravi needs a fast, reliable system to recover lost items without visiting stations.
