SneakScan

Overview: SneakScan aggregates sneaker listings from multiple marketplaces so users can compare prices, track drops, and set alerts — a single place to discover, compare, and confidently buy sneakers.

Problem Statement

Buying sneakers online is fragmented and frustrating. Research showed users routinely hop between apps and websites, encounter inconsistent price data, and often abandon purchases because of high perceived price or unavailable sizes. Many users said, “Prices are different everywhere,” and reported losing confidence when data felt unreliable.

Design Statement

SneakScan is a mobile-first comparison and discovery platform — a "Skyscanner for sneakers" — that centralises listings, surfaces reliable price history, and provides trust indicators and alerts so users can decide quickly and confidently.

Stakeholders: Sneaker buyers, resellers, marketplace partners

Target users: Sneaker enthusiasts, collectors, deal-seekers

Key personas

The Collector

  • Checks multiple resale apps daily
  • Tracks prices manually or with notes
  • Needs accurate price history and authenticity signals

Streetwear / Fashion Enthusiast

  • Follows influencers for drops and styles
  • Buys quickly during hype drops
  • Wants visual discovery and inspiration

Casual Buyer — Deal Seeker

  • Waits for discounts or sales
  • Buys when confident about price and trust
  • Finds too many platforms confusing

Project details

  • Tools: Figma, Photoshop, Miro
  • Timeline: 3 - 6 Months
  • Roles: UX Research, Interaction Design, Prototyping

Design stages

Empathise

User interviews and market analysis to understand shopping behaviour and pain points.

Define

Synthesised insights to define core problems: fragmentation and lack of price visibility.

Ideate

Brainstormed comparison views, alert models, and trust badges for sellers.

Prototype

Interactive Figma prototypes for search, compare, and alert flows.

Testing

Usability tests with sneakerheads to validate scan-to-compare flow and alert UX.

User research & metrics

Key findings: In our research we found widespread frustration with fragmented marketplaces. Nearly 8 out of 10 shoppers reported abandoning a purchase because the price felt too high, and many users said they had to check several apps to confirm stock and price. Survey results (n=48) indicated ~72% would consider using a consolidated comparison app.

Quantitative metrics

  • ~80% of participants experienced cart abandonment due to pricing concerns.
  • About 60% of users missed out on purchases due to stock unavailability in their size.
  • Prototype tests showed a high task success rate when comparison and alerts were clear (pilot test: 85% task success).

Insights

  • Core tasks are smooth & intuitive — users could search, compare, and view details easily.
  • Inconsistent data from marketplaces reduces trust — users want clear sellers and price provenance.
  • Alerts and watchlists lack clear feedback — users were unsure when a saved alert would trigger.
  • Price history and trend indicators help users decide whether to buy now or wait.

What we learned

Users prioritize trust and speed. Clear price provenance, consistent data, and quick visual comparison reduce abandonment and support conversion.

User flows & screens

Search & Discover

Users search for models, apply filters, and discover matches across marketplaces. The discovery view surfaces influencer looks and visual inspirations.

Compare & Decide

Compare prices, view price history, and check seller trust signals to decide whether to buy now or wait.

Watchlist & Alerts

Save searches or items and receive alerts for price drops, restocks, and important release updates.

Redirect to Checkout

Quick redirect to marketplace checkout with clear provenance and seller info.

Primary features

  • Multi-market price comparison
  • Saved searches & alerts
  • Authenticity verification snippets
  • Quick redirect to marketplace checkout
